Our Story

How it strated

Madaraka Dental Centre Ltd is registered under Companies Registration Acts as Madaraka Dental centre Ltd. It is incorporated in Kenya and fully Kenyan owned.

Madaraka Dental Centre was opened on 15th March 2021 and still in operation. The initial staff structure consisted of one dentist, one dental assistant and a receptionist. During the course of the last few months in operation, the staff base has increased to include one more dental practitioner and a support staff. To date, Madaraka Dental Centre has one fully functioning operational dental unit with a dental specialist available for specialized dental care. The Clinic offers affordable dental services to our different classes of clients hence witnessing an increase in the number of patients visiting.

Our Equipment

Fully Functioning Operational Dental Unit

A well maintained and serviced dental unit improves the ability of the dental practitioner to provide a high level of dental care. Infection control is improved by including suction, cuspidor, and an ultrasonic scaler. The chair can be adjusted for treatment ease.

Intra-Oral Camera

Allows the dentist to examine and show the patient close-up views of their teeth. Images can be saved and shared through email, WhatsApp, or other means.

Intra-Oral X-Ray Sensor

Provides fast imagery with low radiation, high-quality digital results, and supports infection control without the use of harmful chemicals.

Endo-Motor Systems

Enables precise root canal cleaning and obturation, improving treatment outcomes and providing better value.

External Suction Unit

Ensures continuous suction of saliva during procedures and serves as a backup in case the main suction system fails.

A smokeless, sterilizable tool for cutting root fillings during endodontic treatment.

Teeth Whitening Machine

A modern system used for effective chemical teeth whitening procedures.

Infection Prevention and Control

The world today reports high prevalence of a number of infectious diseases such as influenza, TB, Hepatitis B, HIV, and the most recent COVID-19 pandemic. To protect our staff and patients, Madaraka Dental Centre strictly adheres to Universal Infection Control procedures that aim to prevent cross infections.

The dentist and the dental assistant wear Personal Protective Equipment (P.P.E.), that includes uniforms changed on a daily basis, disposable gloves, masks, and eye wear. This helps to prevent the spread of infective bacteria and viruses to and from the patient.

The presence of a full-time dental assistant ensures that the dental practitioner is able to focus his/her attention on the patient and in so doing avoiding further cross contamination by allowing the dentist to limit contact with any other surfaces other than those in immediate use with regards the dental treatment being performed.

All work surfaces and non-heat sterilizable material containers that are ‘touched’ and potentially contaminated during a dental treatment are subjected to a chemical wipe with an alcohol-based solution that renders the surfaces disinfected.

All hand instruments that come into contact with bodily fluids, i.e. blood, saliva and plasma or pus, are subjected to a sterilization routine that kills all microorganisms leaving the instruments 100% sterile prior to their use in any other patient. The sterilization routine consists of the following stages:

Click to expand sterilization routine steps
  1. Gross removal of all organic material contaminating the surfaces of equipment/instruments/burs/rubber and plastic instruments through a manual process and with the use of a scouring agent/ brush.
  2. Soak all the equipment/instruments/burs/plastic instruments in a hypo-chloride based solution for 10 minutes. This achieves 100% disinfection.
  3. Soak instruments in soapy water for 20 minutes.
  4. Rinse in clean water and air dry before packaging for hot sterilization.
  5. Aseptic packaging of all previously cold sterilized instruments into individual heat sterilizing packages.
  6. Autoclave/heat sterilization of all metal based instruments/burs at 174 degrees Celsius for 15 minutes. This renders the instruments 100% sterilized.
  7. Correct storage of all sterilized instrument packs in designated drawers.
  8. Opening of sterilized packs when needed only and in the presence of the patient.

All disposable items, such as suction tips, applicator tips, needles and partially used local anesthetic cartridges are correctly discarded after each patient.
